Homeward Bound 2020

I am in! // Je suis sélectionnée!

Posted by Reina Camacho Toro on January 05, 2020

I have been selected for the Homeward Bound 2020 leadership program! I am over-exited about enrolling in #TeamHB2020 to contribute towards gender equality, women in science and collaborative science on globaux change.

What is Homeward Bound?

It is a state of the art leadership and strategic program for women in science from around the globe. I will be part of HB's third cohort, 75 women from at least 25 different countries who will follow a one-year leadership program followed by a three-week trip to Antarctica (planed for November 2020).

HB is about women in STEMM. About global challenges. About becoming a leader. About scientific literacy. About international collaboration. About increasing the visibility of women in STEMM who are changing the world. About the worldwide network of those very same women working together to protect our one and only planet. And sure, it's a little bit about Antarctica. Because Antarctica is a place at the front lines of climate change - what happens there impacts us all. But Antarctica is also a symbol, of scientific exploration and global collaboration. For more information about Homeward Bound click here.

Who is participating?

Last november HB released the official announcement of #TeamHB2020 to the world! Check out the webpage with all our bios listed and this launch video about our leadership vision.

Je m'appelle Reina Camacho Toro, physicienne des particules et diplomate scientifique du Venezuela, vivant et travaillant en France. Je suis membre de l'expérience ATLAS du Grand collisionneur de hadrons (LHC) au CERN et je participe également à plusieurs programmes de development des capacités scientifiques dans les pays en développement avec le soutien du CERN, de l'ICTP et de l'Union Européenne. Mon expérience professionnel m'a appris l'importance de la diversité, du genre ou des origines, si on veut pouvoir faire face aux enjeux futures. Voilà comment je me suis impliqué avec le Homeward Bound Project, un programme de leadership d'un an destiné aux femmes scientifiques, en formant et en mettant en avant les femmes engagées pour trouver des solutions aux défis mondiaux comme la défense de l’environnement et aider à rapprocher la science de la société.
